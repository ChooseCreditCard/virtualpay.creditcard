What is a virtual credit card?

A virtual card is a card number generated in your app or online banking, usually linked to your main account or card, but with its own number, expiry and rules.

  • • Different number than your plastic card
  • • Can be limited to a single merchant or purchase
  • • Easy to freeze or delete without replacing your main card
  • • Ideal for online shopping and subscriptions

Typical Virtual Card Use Cases

One-time purchases

Generate a card number, pay once, then close it.

Subscriptions

Use separate virtual cards for different services and kill them if needed.

Trials

Stop unwanted charges after free trials by closing the virtual card.

Risky merchants

Protect your main card at unfamiliar or low-trust sites.

Business expenses

Issue virtual cards to employees with their own limits.

Geo or merchant-limited cards

Advanced issuers let you restrict region or MCC.

How virtual cards work behind the scenes

Layer Role
Issuer app Generates and displays the virtual card details.
Tokenization Virtual number may itself be a token mapped to your real account.
Limits & rules Spending caps, merchant restrictions, expiry dates.
Authorisation engine Checks whether a transaction fits the virtual card’s rules.
Settlement Final charges still land on your main account or statement.

Benefits of VirtualPay-style cards

  • • Stronger control over who can charge you and when
  • • Reduced impact if one merchant is breached
  • • Cleaner separation of personal, business and subscriptions
  • • Easy to stop specific recurring charges
  • • Works well together with mobile wallets and digital cards
